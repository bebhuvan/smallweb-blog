About

A cozy corner of the web celebrating independent voices

As LLMs continue to devour most of the internet, there's a worry about what this means for good content. Yet despite all the noise about AI slop, there remains a phenomenal hidden substrate of wonderful independent blogs—people publishing amazing perspectives that don't get the attention they deserve.

Most attention has been colonized by YouTube, TikTok, and the bigger publications. But I personally love reading these individual blogs, writers who craft something meaningful without chasing algorithms or engagement metrics.

Indie Blogs is a hand-curated aggregator. Every blog here has been personally read and selected. No algorithms, no AI recommendations—just a human picking things worth reading.

How this works

  • Every blog is hand-picked after reading at least two posts
  • RSS feeds are fetched twice daily automatically
  • No tracking, no ads, no algorithmic sorting
  • Posts are shown chronologically, newest first
  • Open source and free forever

What makes a good indie blog?

It's not about follower counts or posting frequency. A good indie blog has a distinct voice, offers genuine perspective, and cares about craft. The writer thinks for themselves rather than chasing trends.

The best blogs feel like a conversation with a thoughtful friend—someone who's spent time thinking deeply about something and wants to share what they've learned.
